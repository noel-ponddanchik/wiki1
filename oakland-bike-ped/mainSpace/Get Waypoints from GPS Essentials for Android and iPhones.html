Android

To Photograph and Collect GPS Coordinates
  1. Click to open GPS Essentials app
  2. Click 'Camera'
  3. Adjust lighting as desired using the sun icon in the lower left corner of the screen
  4. Take the picture with the shutter button in the lower left corner of the screen
  5. Press the return button the phone
  6. Click 'Waypoints'
  7. Scroll to the bottom to the last photo taken (It will be titled "Picture Location)."
  8. Click that picture's listing.
  9. Change the title to that of the sign's id. When done, hit 'Back'.

Get waypoints and photos from the GPS Essentials app

  1. When back in the office and ready to export, use the usb cable to connect the phone to the computer.
  2. From within the app, in the waypoints menu, use the menu button to pull up the option of exporting.
  3. Click export, then click next the middle button on the screen which has one circle to the left, connected to two other circles.
  4. From within the computer, navigate to the phone's file folders. Within the "Phone" directory, you should see a kml file. This will contain all of the waypoints from the waypoints list within the app. It can be copied and pasted into the temporary photos folder of whatever project is having its signs verified.
  5. The photos will be in the directory "/com.mictale.gpsessentials/images". They can be copied and pasted into the project's temporary photos folder.
  6. Change the file extension of the images to .JPG for signs or .jpg for racks, and make sure they are all rotated so the sign/rack is upright in the photo.
  7. Once the photos are labeled correctly with the appropriate id number of the sign/rack and the appropriate suffix from step 6, move the photo from the temporary folder into the folder with all of the other photos of signs/racks.
  8. Open the kml file in Google Earth and double check to make sure that the points are "reasonably" accurate.
    1. If need be, one can look in Google Earth and using their pointer, identify the coordinates of where the sign is and take note of it.
    2. Then the kml file can be opened in notepad ++ or some other text editor, and the points coordinates can be edited manually inside the placemark's (there is one placemark per waypoint) tag that says "coordinates."
      1. Note that the coordinates are longitude then latitude, and that the number of decimal places used must be 4 or 6.
    3. To convert the coordinates from Google Earth, which are in (degrees, minutes, seconds), to a pure decimal, one can use the formula that the decimal = degrees + (1/60) * minutes + (1/3600) * seconds or one can use a calculator online.
  9. Update the GIS wayfinding sign layer using the kml file. See here for a how-to.

Alternate Instructions for some phones

  1. If clicking on waypoints brings up a photograph, then hit the left arrow near the butterfly next to waypoints.
  2. Hold the waypoints menu option until the top of the screen says “One selected.”
  3. Hit the three vertical dots button on the bottom right hand side of the screen in order to pull up the menu. Then press export.
  4. Click the mail arrow on the right side of the screen, next to the location where the kml file will be stored on your phone.
  5. Plug the phone into the computer and open up the phone’s folder on the computer.
  6. Navigate to com.mictale.gpsessentials and then to temp where you’ll be able to copy or cut the necessary kml file and place it in a more appropriate location.
  7. Update the GIS wayfinding sign layer using the kml file. See here for a how-to.

iPhone (Motion X app)

To Photograph and Collect GPS Coordinates
In order to get KML files exported, each waypoint must be shared (with its accompanying photo) after each waypoint is created.
  1. Stand as close to sign as possible and press 'Menu' ->'My Position'->'Mark Wypt'
  2. Then 'Add a Photo' (Be sure to get the pole and a clear picture of the sign(s) in the frame before hitting the camera button to snap the picture).
  3. Click 'Use Photo' and then 'Done'
  4. Change "Waypoint XXX" to the appropriate sign number by tapping "Waypoint XXX" with your thumb. A Name dialog box will open and you can input the number there.
  5. Tap the three bar menu in the top right-hand corner, and then 'Share' (ensuring the recipient e-mail address is 'ibikeoakland@gmail.com')
  6. Repeat for each point.
