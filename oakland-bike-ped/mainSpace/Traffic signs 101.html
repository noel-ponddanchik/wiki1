Traffic signs are described in the Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices (a good description of the MUTCD is at https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manual_on_Uniform_Traffic_Control_Devices.

California is one of the states that has its own version of the MUTCD. As of September 2015, the last approved version was in 2014: http://www.dot.ca.gov/hq/traffops/engineering/mutcd/ca_mutcd2014.htm. Signs are in Chapter 2. Bike stuff (both signs and striping) are in Chapter 9. Engineers and planners, generally speaking, like to comply w/ the MUTCD unless there is a compelling and documented reason to do otherwise.

Signs fall into the following generally categories:

  • Regulatory, generally black print on a white blade, but also STOP and YIELD signs, as well as No Parking, BIKE LANE, and Street Sweeping signs. These signs indicate laws that you can be fined for breaking.
  • Warning, generally black on yellow.
  • Guide/recreational. Green, sometimes brown. Our wayfinding signs are guide signs.
  • Temporary traffic control, black on orange. These are sometimes versions of the other signs on an orange blade and are used during construction projects to indicate a temporary change to the road condition.

A list of CA approved signs is in this chart: http://www.dot.ca.gov/hq/traffops/engineering/control-devices/signchart/CASIGNCHARTS2014_Tabloid.pdf
