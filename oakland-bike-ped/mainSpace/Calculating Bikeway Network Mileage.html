Calculate Proposed and Existing Lane Mileage

  • In ArcGIS, perform a query on bikeways to isolate all bikeways with lanes on each side of the street
    • this excludes oneway streets, streets with bikeways on oneside of the street, and streets with large center medians that are represented by two streets in the streets layer (i.e. Mandela Parkway)
  • In Select by Attribute, perform a Query on Bikeways: ProposedClass IN ( '1' , '2' , '3' , '3A' , '3B' , '4') AND bikeways.ONEWAY IS NULL
  • In the attribute table, click Show Selected Records button, then right click on Shape_Length and click Statistics
  • Take the Sum, which is in feet and convert it to miles (use google or divide by 5280)
  • Multiply this total by 2 for the lane mileage of all bikeways with lanes on both sides of the street
  • In Select by Attribute, perform a Query on Bikeways: ProposedClass <> '0' AND ProposedClass = '2.1' OR bikeways.ONEWAY IS NOT NULL
  • right click on Shape_Length and click Statistics, take the Sum and convert to miles
  • Add the total from the bikeways with two lanes and those with only one for the total bikeway lane mileage
