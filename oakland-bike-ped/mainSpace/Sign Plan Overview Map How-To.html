The first draft overview map provides a first glance at destinations that will be supported along the route with mileages on confirmation signs and off-route destinations at decision points/bikeway junctions. This gets distributed for discussion amongst the staff, and brings the project to 5% complete in the tracking spreadsheet. Once comments and edits are incorporated into the map, the project reaches 15% completion of the sign planning process and work on the first draft of the sign plan can begin.


The overview map helps us visualize how the corridor integrates with intersecting bikeways, which is important later when deciding on sign messages for decision points along the corridor.

  • In ArcMap, open the BikewayOverview.mxd \\oakland\pwa\Transportation\Bike-Ped_Program\GIS\signage\BikewayOverview.mxd
  • Save a copy in the corridor file

  • If your symbology is lost, refer to Importing Symbology
  • Zoom to your corridor and consider the extent in Layout View
  • For most corridors, showing the entire city is unnecessary, but the idea is to “zoom out” a bit to see some of those corridor-to-corridor connections
  • Once satisfied with the extent, create a bookmark for it and title it after your corridor
  • Select your corridor in the "Corridor" layer and symbolize only your corridor so it appears yellow on the map

Export Overview Map to Illustrator

Do as much work as possible in GIS before exporting to Illustrator (as shown below). It is extremely time-consuming to go back to GIS once you enter the Illustrator phase. (Also, exporting to AI can be buggy, work-a-rounds from http://www.gsd.harvard.edu/gis/manual/illustrator/.)
  • Turn off any layers you do not want exported by unchecking their box in the ‘Table of Contents’
  • Make sure raster layers are at the bottom of the order
  • Once your map is prepped, select from the ‘File’ menu ‘Export Map’
  • Choose ‘AI’ from the ‘Save as Type’ pull down
  • Click ‘Options’ button on lower left corner of export window
  • Navigate to ‘General Options’ tab and set DPI to 200
  • Navigate to ‘Format Options’ tab to set the ‘Destination Colorspace:’ to ‘CMYK’ and the ‘Picture Symbol’ pull-down to ‘Vectorize Bitmap or Picture Symbol Fills’
  • Make sure the ‘Convert Marker Symbols to Polygons’ is checked
  • Click ‘Save’
  • Open in Illustrator and click ‘Update’ to convert the fonts; Select Text Objects, then arrow key left twice and right twice. Resave in pdf format and begin editing.
  • The Adobe Illustrator Legend Template is located at \\oakland\pwa\Transportation\Bike-Ped_Program\Projects\Bikeways-signage\1-resources\work-orders-general
  • In Adobe Illustrator make sure File>Document Color Mode> CMYK Color is selected and that the Color Panel is also set to CMYK by selecting the drop down in the upper right hand corner of the color panel
  • Save the file as an Illustrator-editable PDF in Illustrator
  • The Illustrator file can then be deleted

Select the Destinations that will Appear on Decision Signs

Decision signs (those that include destination names and turn arrows) appear before a turn from one bikeway street to onto another bikeway street (e.g. the turn from northbound Webster St onto 48th St). There are often many possible choices of which destinations to select.
  1. Consult the Bicycle Master Plan and pages 7-8 of the Wayfinding Guidelines \\oakland\pwa\Transportation\Bike-Ped_Program\Design_Guidance-Oakland\3-Wayfinding-Guidance\Design_Guidelines\OaklandBicycleWayfindingGuidelines_2017.pdf
    1. Pages 7-8 give instructions on choosing destinations
    2. Pages 11-14 list the possible destinations
    3. Page 15 has a map of the destinations
In general, determine the closest destination the bikeway points to, up to 2 miles for a secondary destination or up to 4 miles for a primary destination and try to choose local destinations over farther ones.

Notes: This is just the starting point. Sometimes, the destinations chosen through this selection process don’t make good sense in practice and may be changed during the wayfinding sign development process.
