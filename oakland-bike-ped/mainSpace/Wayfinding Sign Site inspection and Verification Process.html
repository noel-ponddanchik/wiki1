  • Pre-install, export Field Review report/post-install copy Attachment A, and take map (Attachment C), and removal list (Attachment D) from relevant project in \\oakland\pwa\Transportation\Bike-Ped_Program\Projects\Bikeways-signage
  • Make sure you know what each sign type looks like
  • Map or plot out route if needed/desired
  • Mentally prepare yourself for returning to a location with no great options (after reviewing possibilities with staff)--take lots of photos (since Google maps won't always have the right angle)
  • Take clip board or binder; pen/pencil(s); camera; measuring wheel; street map (if needed); reflective vest; copy of guidelines; copy of striping plan (to ensure that sign locations conform to guidelines—alternatively, make relevant notes on map); spray paint

Site inspections (new signs):

Pole types

  • First choice: electroliers
  • Do not install on:
    • traffic signal poles or utility poles (note: some wooden poles are electroliers, but most are utility poles and cannot be used)
    • poles that have more than one other sign (unless they can be re-hung flanking like SSS/2-hr signs)
    • poles with speed limit or stop signs

Locations for new poles

  • Apply siting criteria from guidelines
  • Avoid the following areas:
    • o directly adjacent to in pavement utilities or across from utilities that are mounted on the side of a building
    • o within 4’ of driveways or perpendicular walkways
    • o directly outside residential windows (that may obstruct views)
    • o cracked concrete (unless absolutely necessary)
    • o near trees that are likely to obscure sign now or in the medium-term future
  • Good areas include:
    • In planter strip (if it exists) rather than concrete—but avoid obviously laboriously tended garden areas
    • On the property line between buildings
  • Photograph locations when there is a question; save photo into signage project street folder titled ID#.jpg to review with staff

Documentation for work orders

  • Document the existing pole type (round/square) or electrolier number and whether existing signs on the proposed pole are faded or otherwise damaged, or need to be rehung
  • Mark locations on curb with ^ and sign ID #
  • Note street address whenever possible and business name where applicable

Work order:

  • For uncertain locations, discuss with Jennifer.
  • Database/map: change sign location based on field work including changing the cross street (confirmation signs only), changing near- to far-side, etc.
  • Compute mileage using http://www.gmap-pedometer.com. For complicated routes, document assumptions in overview workbook. Perform QA/QC by comparing the distances on confirmation signs to make sure they are consistent as the route progresses. Update database/map.
  • Database, installer instructions field, write, in order*:
    1. pole information (new, round, square, electrolier #, etc.)
    2. address/location information (use street address even if on cross-street); description should refer to direction of travel (before/past; beginning/end)—avoid ambiguous words (e.g. back/front)
    3. mounting information (e.g. “above SSS,” or “below ped sign—raise so that bottom of bike assembly is at 8.5 ft”)
    4. other (note round poles and damaged signs but do not include instructions to replace—this is for Traffic Maintenance to decide)
  • Input/update quantities into database fields. Assume that round poles will be replaced.
  • Run quantities report and enter into relevant overview workbook; perform quick QA/QC by counting signs on map.
  • As needed, populate removal list and note needed changes on map. Print overview, Attachment A, and removal list on draft paper for Jennifer’s review. Make changes as needed and finalize these and map to submit to Jason.

* Why in order? This makes it more likely that all the needed information will be provided, and it's easier to review.

Field verification (post-installation):

  • Verify that sign was placed according to installer instructions
  • Document any problems being sure to review for all of the following:

Installation/removal related
  1. wrong location
  2. too low/too high
  3. gap between blades
  4. signs not removed


Design related
  1. wrong message or incorrect arrows/logos
  2. too much or not enough space between logos and text and/or edge of sign
  3. bad location choice by us

  • Photograph and get GPS coordinate for each point using mobile App (Collector, or GPS Essentials for Android or Motion X for iPhone);
    1. Repeat the process for that of all the signs that need to have a waypoint recorded for them (specific device how-to’s in link above).

    • create punch list (if needed) in overview workbook;
    • update the Access database to reflect your field notes;
    • using ArcMap ensure that the points in the signage layer of the master geodatabase reflect your field verifications. See the following how-to for details.
    • label photos with database ID# and.JPG suffix.
  • If punch list is submitted, schedule time to verify after installed.
